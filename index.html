Welcome to the National Development Summit of Traditional Rulers (NDSTR)

When the vision of National Development Summit of Traditional Rulers came in 2008, we were quite mindful that the road to success runs uphill, so we didn't expect to break any speed records. Yet we knew that hard work was the easiest bridge to cross over to success. Today, we look at the Summit in retrospect with a sense of fruition and triumph. Triumph over pessimism, opposition and teething glitches. We may not have attained but we have certainly crossed the Rubicon. So far, we thank God for the fruit of labour and thought.

The Summit was inaugurated by His Excellency, former President and Commander-in-Chief of the Armed Forces of Nigeria, late Alhaji Umaru Musa Yar'Adua, on August 12, 2008, at Yar'Adua Center, Abuja. It has remained a dominant interactive and unifying forum of traditional rulers in Nigeria all this while, attracting scores of delegates from all the States/Local Government Areas yearly.

The core objective of the Summit is to provide a forum for stakeholders to interact and foster friendship in the interest of national unity, peace and development. The Summit also seeks to initiate a conceptual framework for the restoration of societal cherished values and cultures in the interest of national refinement. Every session is designed to be highly interactive, bringing together experts, policy makers, implementers, as well as opinion and community leaders nation-wide to brainstorm on topical issues of public interest.

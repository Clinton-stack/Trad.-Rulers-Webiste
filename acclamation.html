ACCLAMATION

- Late President Umaru Musa Yardua - "The holding of this summit at this time is a clear testimony to your constancy, your abiding faith in Nigeria, and your patriotic resolve to be effective partners in our journey of national regeneration."(2008)

- DG NOA, Dr Garba Abari - "I will like to commend the organizers of this great gathering for this initiative as I urge us all to participate and be active and as well contribute generously to the discourse as it is my belief the deliberations will yield fruitful and robust results. (2016)"

- Dr Chukwuemeka Ezeife, Former Governor of Anambra State (2008) - "I commend and congratulate everybody who has responded to this call for the development of Nigeria."

- Former The Hon. Minister Of The Federal Capital Territory Aliyu Modibbo Umar, PHD (2008) - "It is our hope that this summit shall come up with ideas that would meaningfully reposition and strengthen the third-tier of government in the country for optimal service delivery. I charge this summit to also explore plausible means and strategies for performance monitoring of government programmes and fiscal appropriation for the local governments."

- Late H.R.H. Bello Asuku Ibrahim, Phd, Former Ohi of Adavi, Kogi State and Member, Governing Council NDSTR (2013) - " To all intents and purposes, the National Development Summit of Traditional Rulers is a forum of giants. I recommend the National Development Summit of Traditional Rulers to all well meaning traditional rulers in Nigeria, as an avenue to collaborate with their colleagues nation-wide in the task of nation building. We have no other nation we can call our own. Holding aloof is unethical. Let's build friendship and useful linkages for harmony and peace. Nigeria can be better than what it is if all the custodians of our cultures, ethics and values shall wake up to serve without waiting for fair weather. No man is wise enough by himself. It is real fun coming together periodically to share ideas, exchange pleasantries and see diverse royal insignias and colours."

ABOUT US

The National Development Summit of Traditional Rulers (NDSTR) is co-hosted by the National Orientation Agency (NOA) and Traditional Rulers Development and Welfare Initiative (TRDWI), while the official facilitator is the Center for Traditional Rulers Advancement (CTRA).

This is not just another forum. This is a very unique and thoroughgoing body of certified and decent category of traditional rulers in Nigeria, as nominated by their various states governments and local communities. It is demonstratively a patriotic union with unwavering commitment to collective action for societal refinement, peace, unity and progress.

The Summit was inaugurated by His Excellency, former President and Commander-in-Chief of the Armed Forces of Nigeria, late Alhaji Umaru Musa Yar'Adua, on August 12, 2008, at Yar'Adua Center, Abuja. It has remained a dominant interactive and unifying forum of traditional rulers in Nigeria all this while, attracting scores of delegates from all the States and Local Government Areas yearly.
Beyond fostering constructive interaction and cross-cultural friendships, the Summit also seeks to initiate a conceptual framework for the restoration of the fading societal cherished values and cultures.

Every edition of the Summit is designed to be highly edifying, bringing together experts, policy makers, implementers, as well as opinion and community leaders nation-wide, to brainstorm on topical issues of public interest.
More than ever, the Summit has successfully created and strengthened effective networking among the stakeholders, with enormous advantages and opportunities. Under the prevailing social circumstance in the country, the Summit has been very helpful in dousing the scourge of tribal bitterness, religious acrimony, terrorism and kidnapping.

In the fourth year of the Summit, Monarchs Magazine was born as a mouth piece of the Summit. This annual publication has endeared itself to a wide audience due to its regular rich contents. It was conceived to serve as a medium for free and objective expression of thoughts, ideas and feelings on matters of monarchical traditional rulership system vis-Ã -vis modern democratic local government, with a view to repositioning grassroots administration for optimal performance. Six editions of this famous magazine have been read.

SUMMIT CARDINAL OBJECTIVES

1. To provide a forum for stakeholders to interact and foster friendship towards the making of a stronger, just and egalitarian society, with bright opportunities for all

2. To proffer ideas on how to maximize the potentials of traditional rulers in grassroots administration through constitutional empowerment

Expectedly, the size of participants has increased tremendously from less than 100 at the onset to over 300 lately, with delegates coming from almost all the states of the federation. The Summit holds every traditional ruler in high esteem and does not create room for unnecessary rivalries. The Summit admits strictly only traditional rulers duly installed and acknowledged by their communities.

The summit has given all our participants the rare opportunity of establishing friendships beyond their ethnic enclaves. Go to any carnivals and coronation anniversaries in all the six geographical zones of the federation, you will find a large number of visiting traditional rulers from various tribes adding glamour to the events. This is part of the advantages of the Summit.
Mindful of the intrinsic worth of traditional rulership, the Summit has been at the forefront of the campaign to give traditional rulers constitutional roles in Nigeria like in many other African nations. The Summit is convinced that there can be no better way to maximize the leadership potentials of traditional rulers than to entrench their position in the constitution and fund their palaces adequately. The Summit is gladdened that the National Assembly has commenced work on the review of the federal constitution but wonders at the snail speed of the process.

The Summit commends the tenacity, cooperation, understanding, sacrifice and patriotism of all royal authorities who have identified with the Summit all this period.
We have come a long way. The time to intensify effort is now, till we see the materialization of the Nigeria of our dream.

In 2013, the Summit published her FIRST COMPENDIUM, a rare compilation of the lectures by some of the best brains in diverse and relevant disciplines, delivered at various occasions of the Summit, including the speeches presented by heads of significant government ministries and institutions at the Summit in her first five years. Copies of this enriching anthology are still available at the Summit secretariat, Abuja. The second compendium is due by 2019, covering the summit activities in ten years, 2008 to 2018. The Summit could not convene in 2017 due the unfavourable economic situation in the country.
